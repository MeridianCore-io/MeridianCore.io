What it does

TheMrLurker v6.4 runs in the background of Chrome, Edge, or Firefox as a Manifest V3 extension. Open the popup, point it at a channel or campaign list, and it handles the session from there — no babysitting required.

The core use case is Twitch Drops: staying connected to streams long enough to claim time-based rewards. TheMrLurker manages that connection automatically, handles the claim step when drops complete, and moves to the next campaign in your queue without intervention.

Session Management

Sessions are time-bounded and configurable. Define a squad (list of favorite channels to prefer), and the extension handles rotation when streams go offline or raids redirect you somewhere you didn’t ask to go. Anti-raid routing keeps you on the channels you chose, not wherever the streamer decided to send their audience.

A per-session time limit (premium feature) can be set to automatically stop lurking after a defined number of hours.

Mod View Support

TheMrLurker v6.4 passively detects channels where you are a moderator by reading the page DOM — no setup required. When you launch a squad channel you moderate, the extension automatically opens mod view instead of regular view. A MOD badge on squad entries marks confirmed mod channels.

The Current tab shows active session state. Dashboard manages your squad list and campaign queue. Stats tracks historical drop claims and session hours. The Drops tab manages your drop rotation queue — the Drop Campaign button lets you paste any Twitch drops page URL to automatically extract participating streamers and add them to your queue; the extension handles rotation and timing from there. The ⚙ Settings tab controls trial activation, license key entry, session limits, and quality preferences.

Quality Control

Stream quality is auto-set to 360p or lower — the extension targets 360p specifically, with fallback to lower resolution if unavailable. You’re not watching the stream; you’re farming drops. Reducing quality reduces bandwidth consumption for sessions that may run for hours. This is configurable if you want to set a different quality floor.

Technical Notes

Built entirely in vanilla JavaScript with no build system, no bundler, and no external runtime dependencies. All session state is stored locally in the browser’s extension storage — nothing leaves your machine.

Manifest V3 compliance means the extension uses a service worker background script rather than a persistent background page, in line with current Chrome Web Store and Firefox AMO requirements.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026. TheMrLurker does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. Your squad list, settings, statistics, and license state are stored locally on your device using browser extension storage. They never leave your device.

When you use the Drop Campaign feature, the extension fetches the URL you provide to extract channel names — this request goes directly from your browser to that URL; nothing is logged by us.

Permissions used: storage (local data only) — tabs (open and manage Twitch tabs) — scripting (automate the Twitch video player) — activeTab (detect current tab URL) — downloads (export squad backup) — optional host permissions (fetch drop campaign pages you explicitly request).
